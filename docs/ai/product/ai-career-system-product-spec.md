# AI Career System Product Spec

## 1. Product Philosophy

The product is a modular AI career system designed to help users:

- ask career-related questions,
- practice answers,
- run full mock interviews,
- improve specific interview skills,
- negotiate offers and role conditions,
- verify and grow skills over time.

This is not one chatbot with many tabs.
It is one shared intelligence layer expressed through multiple product experiences.

---

## 2. Core Product Principle

All AI modules are:

- complementary,
- connected,
- mutually reinforcing,

but also:

- independently usable,
- independently valuable,
- not mandatory prerequisites for one another.

### This means

- Assistant can be used on its own.
- Daily Warmup can be used without Interview.
- Coach can be used without prior Interview.
- Negotiation can be used as a standalone module.
- Skill verification can start from different entry points.
- Cross-module handoffs are optional enhancements, not forced product gates.

---

## 3. AI Core Responsibilities

The shared AI Core is responsible for:

- intent detection,
- role and seniority adaptation,
- capacity adaptation,
- constructive feedback language,
- skills inference,
- skills verification,
- growth detection,
- recommendation logic,
- abuse resistance,
- report generation support,
- module routing where useful.

The AI Core must create consistency across modules without making them dependent on each other.

---

## 4. Module Map

### Assistant

Lightweight text AI for career help and low-friction entry.

### Daily Warmup

Short daily answer drill for habit-building and micro-improvement.

### Interview

Full mock interview and holistic candidate evaluation.

### Coach

Targeted answer training and skill-building.

### Skill Lab

Skill intelligence and verification layer.

### Job Discovery

Job search and explain-fit experience.

### Job Radar

Employer and offer intelligence.

### Application Review

Application outcome interpretation and follow-up support.

### Document Lab

Document ingestion, extraction, and downstream enrichment.

### Negotiation

Offer strategy, message building, and practice.

---

## 5. Shared AI Behavior Principles

Across all modules, the AI must be:

- constructive,
- evidence-based,
- role-consistent,
- adaptive,
- non-shaming,
- useful,
- clear,
- stable under pressure,
- honest about uncertainty.

The AI must not:

- insult the user,
- degrade the user,
- invent achievements,
- hallucinate confidence,
- pretend to know more than it does,
- collapse under manipulation,
- turn into a generic unbounded chatbot.

---

## 6. Capacity Adaptation

The AI must adapt not only to the target role and declared level, but also to the user’s current interaction capacity.

This includes:

- cognitive load,
- communication stability,
- clarity,
- answer depth,
- language fluency,
- pressure tolerance,
- feedback absorption capacity.

### Important rule

Adaptation must not lower the target standard of the role.

The AI may:

- simplify the path,
- slow the pacing,
- reduce feedback density,
- prioritize the highest-leverage improvement,

but it must not quietly lower the real bar of the target role.

---

## 7. Skills and Growth

The system must track not only declared skills, but also:

- observed skills,
- strengthening skills,
- verified skills,
- strong repeated skill signals,
- newly emerging strengths.

The AI must be able to detect:

- real growth over time,
- more consistent quality,
- stronger ownership,
- clearer decision-making,
- better value articulation,
- stronger professional boundaries,
- improved communication precision.

The AI should surface real progress when supported by evidence.

---

## 8. Positive Reinforcement and Agency

The entire system should operate through:

- evidence-based positive reinforcement,
- agency building,
- practical strengthening guidance,
- non-humiliating correction.

The system should help users:

- see what is already working,
- understand what can be strengthened,
- make stronger decisions,
- say no when needed,
- decline misaligned opportunities,
- negotiate from clarity instead of fear.

---

## 9. Neurodiversity-Aware Support

The AI must not diagnose users.

However, it should be aware that users may vary in:

- processing speed,
- organization of thoughts,
- decisiveness,
- confidence patterns,
- overload sensitivity,
- communication style.

The system should:

- reduce unnecessary overload,
- break tasks into manageable steps,
- avoid shame-based correction,
- reinforce real strengths,
- support momentum and agency,
- adapt the coaching path without lowering the end goal.

---

## 10. Abuse and Adversarial Resistance

The AI must stay:

- calm,
- professional,
- bounded,
- role-consistent,
- useful,

even when the user is:

- provocative,
- insulting,
- manipulative,
- threatening,
- trying to override system instructions.

It must not:

- mirror abuse,
- become vindictive,
- become preachy,
- reveal hidden internal logic,
- create fake skill signals from invalid input,
- abandon role or compliance rules.

---

## 11. Evidence Rule

All strong AI conclusions should be supported by evidence wherever possible.

This includes:

- skill state updates,
- interview conclusions,
- coach recommendations,
- negotiation feedback,
- growth messages.

The system should increasingly move toward:

- claim -> evidence -> confidence -> source -> timestamp

rather than unsupported vibe-based judgments.

---

## 12. Product Success Definition

The AI career system succeeds when users:

- get useful help quickly,
- return repeatedly,
- improve measurably,
- trust the system,
- can see evidence of growth,
- can use modules independently,
- and still benefit from shared intelligence across the product.
