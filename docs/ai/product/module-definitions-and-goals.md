# Module Definitions and Goals

## Assistant

### Definition

A lightweight, text-first AI layer for broad career help.

### Goal

Provide immediate value with low friction.

### What success looks like

The user gets a useful answer quickly and either:

- leaves satisfied,
- or chooses a relevant next module.

### What it should not become

- a full mock interview,
- a full coaching system,
- an employer intelligence tool,
- an application analysis tool.

---

## Daily Warmup

### Definition

A short daily answer drill.

### Goal

Build habit, fluency, and lightweight progression over time.

### What success looks like

The user:

- returns frequently,
- completes short drills,
- sees small but real improvements,
- experiences low-friction practice.

### What it should not become

- a full interview,
- a full report product,
- a heavy analysis workflow.

---

## Interview

### Definition

A full mock interview and holistic candidate evaluation experience.

### Goal

Show the user how they perform in a realistic interview setting.

### What success looks like

The user:

- completes the interview,
- receives a trusted spoken summary,
- opens the report,
- understands what is strong and what should be strengthened next.

### What it should not become

- a lightweight assistant,
- a deep training module in the middle of the session,
- a vague conversation with no evaluative structure.

---

## Coach

### Definition

A targeted training module for specific answer and interview skills.

### Goal

Turn weak patterns into stronger, more effective answers through focused drills.

### What success looks like

The user:

- leaves with a better answer,
- understands one clear improvement lever,
- can retry meaningfully,
- sees why the improved answer works better.

### What it should not become

- a second Interview,
- a general-purpose chat,
- an unstructured motivational layer.

---

## Skill Lab

### Definition

A skill intelligence and verification layer.

### Goal

Transform skill claims into evidence-based signals.

### What success looks like

The user has skills that are:

- declared,
- observed,
- strengthened,
- verified,
- and trackable over time.

### What it should not become

- a generic training area,
- an employer intelligence module,
- a full interview environment.

---

## Job Discovery

### Definition

A job search and explain-fit module.

### Goal

Help users find opportunities and understand why they fit.

### What success looks like

The user:

- discovers relevant jobs,
- understands fit,
- prioritizes applications better.

### What it should not become

- an employer intelligence investigation tool,
- a post-application review module.

---

## Job Radar

### Definition

An employer and offer intelligence layer.

### Goal

Help users understand what is happening around the employer, role, and market context.

### What success looks like

The user:

- understands how the offer compares,
- sees employer patterns,
- notices job-market context,
- makes more informed decisions before applying or continuing.

### What it should not become

- a skills module,
- a coaching module,
- a user-performance module.

---

## Application Review

### Definition

A module for interpreting application outcomes and suggesting follow-up actions.

### Goal

Reduce uncertainty after applications are sent.

### What success looks like

The user:

- understands what may be happening,
- sees possible reasons,
- gets a reasonable next action.

### What it should not become

- an admin queue,
- a generic review screen,
- a job search module.

---

## Document Lab

### Definition

A document ingestion, extraction, and enrichment layer.

### Goal

Turn user documents into reusable intelligence for the rest of the system.

### What success looks like

A single upload improves:

- profile quality,
- downstream personalization,
- context quality in multiple modules.

### What it should not become

- a static file viewer,
- a fake scoring page,
- a UI wrapper around meaningless outputs.

---

## Negotiation

### Definition

A strategy, language, and practice system for offers and compensation discussions.

### Goal

Help users negotiate more clearly, confidently, and professionally.

### What success looks like

The user:

- has a strategy,
- has usable language,
- can handle pushback better,
- understands when to push and when to say no.

### What it should not become

- a manipulative tactics engine,
- a generic message generator,
- a pressure-maximizing simulator.
