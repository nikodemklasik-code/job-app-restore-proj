# Community Goodwill

## Ethos

Community Goodwill is voluntary.

A person contributes because they want to.
A person uses access because it is available.

The platform applies its allocation rules.
The community accepts the outcome of those rules.
The person using access decides whether and how to use it.

Contribution does not create control.
Use does not create debt.

Three principles guide the system:

1. Be human when using Community Goodwill.
2. When contributing, accept that goodwill leaves your hands and enters the community.
3. When using covered access, do so on your own responsibility.

Community Goodwill should not require personal stories, gratitude performance or proof of moral worth.

Goodwill moves through the community.
People remain equal.

## Product Language

Use positive, dignity-preserving language.

Preferred product terms:

- Community Goodwill
- Good Luck Credits
- Covered by Community Goodwill
- Add Goodwill
- Pass it forward
- Goodwill available

Avoid dependency or hierarchy language:

- sponsor
- beneficiary
- hardship case
- vulnerable user
- aid recipient
- donor-recipient relationship
- support case
- your patron
- your sponsor

The product should not frame Community Goodwill as charity, rescue, personal debt, social debt, obligation, repayment, dependence or personal control.

## Contribution Rule

A person contributes because they want to.

A contribution enters the Community Goodwill flow and stops being a personal claim over another person.

Contributors do not receive:

- control over users;
- gratitude rights;
- progress reports;
- recipient identity;
- profile access;
- CV access;
- job-search activity;
- learning progress;
- legal activity;
- AI outputs;
- private user data.

Contributor-facing reporting should be aggregate and privacy-preserving.

Allowed reporting examples:

- Interview practices covered;
- Skill Lab actions covered;
- Legal Hub searches covered;
- reports generated;
- credits used in aggregate;
- remaining goodwill balance in aggregate.

Recipient identities and private activity must not be shown.

## Use Rule

A person uses access because it is available.

Using covered access does not create shame, debt, hierarchy or obligation.

The person using access remains independent and decides whether and how to use the product action.

Using Community Goodwill does not require:

- a personal story;
- proof of moral worth;
- visible gratitude;
- contact with a contributor;
- progress reporting to a contributor;
- repayment;
- endorsement;
- loyalty;
- disclosure of private circumstances.

Abuse of the system may lead to restrictions.

## Allocation Rule

The platform may apply allocation rules to decide when Community Goodwill can cover a product action.

Allocation rules should be transparent, privacy-preserving and designed around access availability, product cost and fair use.

The system should avoid labels that rank personal worth, hardship or vulnerability.

Use neutral internal labels where needed:

- access coverage eligibility;
- goodwill availability;
- covered action eligibility;
- fair-use status;
- usage limit status.

Avoid labels such as poverty score, desperation score, hardship rank or vulnerable user.

## Community Opportunity Rule

Community may include announcements, NGO opportunities, foundation posts, volunteering, internships, work placements, traineeships and practical experience offers.

Unpaid participation is allowed only when it is genuine volunteering.

Allowed unpaid categories:

- genuine volunteering;
- charity volunteering;
- NGO volunteering;
- community support role;
- non-commercial community project.

Not eligible if unpaid:

- commercial internship;
- unpaid work placement for a business;
- unpaid trial shift;
- unpaid practical work where an employer earns from the output;
- unpaid role disguised as learning while replacing paid labour.

If a business commercially benefits from the work, the person must be paid.

Community must not dress exploitation as experience.

Required UI labels:

- Paid;
- Unpaid volunteer;
- Not eligible if unpaid.

Required warning:

> This appears to be unpaid work for a commercial organisation. MultivoHub does not promote unpaid commercial labour. If a business benefits from the work, the person should be paid.

## Cost Coverage Rule

Community Goodwill covers product usage costs for eligible actions.

When a covered action is used, the relevant product cost may be deducted from the Community Goodwill balance according to the product's pricing, reservation and settlement rules.

The platform must not add profit to covered actions.

Pool deductions may cover only the cost required to provide, operate, protect and settle the covered product action, including relevant AI usage, compute, storage, bandwidth, payment processing, fraud prevention, moderation, support and compliance overhead directly connected to covered access.

All movements should be auditable through durable ledger events.

## Privacy Rule

Community Goodwill must not expose private user data to contributors.

Contributor reporting must remain aggregate unless the user has explicitly opted into a clearly defined separate disclosure flow.

Default position:

- no recipient names;
- no CVs;
- no profiles;
- no job activity;
- no documents;
- no legal searches;
- no AI results;
- no support score;
- no personal circumstances.

## Bottom Disclaimer Pattern

Operational notes should live at the bottom of the relevant card, screen or modal.

Example:

> Community Goodwill covers product usage costs for eligible actions. It does not create personal claims, debt, hierarchy, reporting duties or access to private user data. Covered actions are settled through the product cost model. The platform does not add profit to covered actions.

Keep the main UI positive and clean. Put methodology, limitations, cost coverage and privacy details at the bottom.
